## 1. Core narrative spine

The investigation repeatedly separates four layers:

1. What physically happened
2. What the official record claims happened
3. Which permission the system accepted
4. Who actually used that permission

The governing principle remains:

**A valid credential proves access, not identity.**

## 2. Chapter I - Room 1807

- The victim in Room 1807 is not Daniel Voss.
- Room 1807 establishes the method of physical staging.
- The body was moved, the room was arranged, the laptop history was cleared and the phone was placed to be found.
- The unknown caller knows which room Benedict and North investigated.
- `R.`, the pier meeting and the meaning of `1807` remain unresolved.
- Chapter I must not identify the operator or connect Elena to the crime.

## 3. Chapter II - Daniel Voss

- Daniel is a different victim.
- His case repeats the Room 1807 method in a new layer: records and chronology are staged instead of only the physical scene.

- The same eleven-minute anomaly later appears in Daniel's own laboratory record.

## 4. Temporary profile 18-07

`18-07` is a temporary operational role profile. It is not a person's name and does not itself identify the operator.

Confirmed uses:

- 05:47 - Daniel's building access system accepts profile 18-07 under resident-access permission.
- 06:09 - Profile 18-07 carries accepted Evidence Division permission when `COLLECTION_TIME` is changed from 05:58 to 06:09.
- The 06:09 event claims workstation `FS-12`.
- Bangkok personnel also call FS-12 `Terminal Three`.
- FS-12's local session was offline and its network module had been removed.
- The event later resolves through Singapore infrastructure.

The system proves that accepted permission was used. It does not prove the operator or the true point of origin.
